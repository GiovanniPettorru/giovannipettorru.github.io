Biography

Giovanni Pettorru is a Ph.D. in Electronic and Computer Engineering from the University of Cagliari (2026). His research focuses on GPS-free localization, network security, and energy-efficient IoT systems — with a particular emphasis on trustworthy Location-Based Services for next-generation networks across the Fog-Edge-Cloud continuum.

He has authored over 20 peer-reviewed publications in IEEE journals and conferences, and his work spans topics including QUIC-based secure communications, RSS-based localization, traffic classification via CNNs, and IoT security.

Beyond research, Giovanni translates technology into impact: his projects AGROS, LENS, and GreenShield have earned competitive recognition at national and international level, including a Silicon Valley acceleration through the SERICS Lab2Bay program at the INNOVIT hub.

Giovanni Pettorru est Docteur en Génie Électronique et Informatique de l'Université de Cagliari (2026). Ses recherches portent sur la localisation sans GPS, la sécurité des réseaux et les systèmes IoT à faible consommation, avec un accent particulier sur les services de localisation fiables pour les réseaux de nouvelle génération dans le continuum Fog-Edge-Cloud.

Il a publié plus de 20 articles évalués par des pairs dans des revues et conférences IEEE. Ses travaux couvrent les communications sécurisées basées sur QUIC, localisation via RSS, la classification du trafic par CNN, et la sécurité IoT.

Au-delà de la recherche, Giovanni transforme la technologie en impact : ses projets AGROS, LENS et GreenShield ont obtenu des distinctions nationales et internationales, dont une accélération en Silicon Valley via le programme SERICS Lab2Bay au hub INNOVIT.

Giovanni Pettorru received a B.Sc. degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from the University of Cagliari in 2020, with a thesis on the Implementation of a Parking Occupancy Detection System Based on Magnetometer Sensor. In 2022, he earned a Master's Degree in Internet Engineering from the same university, presenting a thesis titled Secure and Low-Latency Communications Based on WebSocket and QUIC in the Internet of Things Scenario. In February 2026, he earned his Ph.D. in Electronic and Computer Engineering from the University of Cagliari, within the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (DIEE/UdR CNIT). His doctoral thesis, titled Energy-Efficient and Trustworthy Location-Based Services for Next-Generation IoT, focused on network security and GPS-free localization strategies in green IoT environments.

Giovanni is an active member of IEEE. He has demonstrated exceptional communication skills by placing third in the 1st Three-Minute Thesis (3MT) competition at IEEE ICC 2023 and participating as one of the 15 finalists in the Four-Minute Thesis (4MT) at IEEE Globecom 2024 and 2025. He has also contributed to the scientific community by serving as a PC member at the AQ-IoT 2023 conference, as Web and Social Media Chair for the IEEE ComSoc Italy Chapter (2026–present), and as a member of the Organizing Committee for IEEE MeditCom 2026.

Giovanni made significant contributions to the POSIDONIA project (POSition Information with Digital twin Offloading in trustworthy Next-generation Internet Applications). Within the Next-Generation Internet (NGI) vision, this project tackles green and human-centric network design across the Fog-Edge-Cloud continuum, focusing on a distributed architecture for reliable GPS-free localization to support Location-Based Services (LBS) in IoT applications. This strong technological foundation fueled his entrepreneurial vision. AGROS earned an award from the University of Cagliari (e.INS Project) and achieved a top 10 finish at Start Cup 2024 Sardegna, followed by recognition as one of the top 10 ideas in the AgriFuture program (Sardegna Ricerche). His project LENS achieved a top 10 finish at Start Cup 2025 and received the "Climate Change" special mention. Most recently, GreenShield was selected for the SERICS Lab2Bay program, granting him entry into an exclusive acceleration path in Silicon Valley at the INNOVIT hub.
